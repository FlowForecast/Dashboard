Insights

Clouds

Little to no shades Means Clear, Sunny Skies - Denser shades Means cooler, overcast skies

Cloud coverage affects temperature, visibility, and weather conditions. Denser clouds result in cooler, overcast skies, while minimal clouds lead to clear, sunny weather. Displayed in shades of white and gray, cloud data helps predict storms and temperature changes, crucial for aviation and outdoor planning.

Precipitation

Light Heavy

Precipitation reflects the amount of water falling as rain, snow, or hail, influencing flood risks, storm intensity, and droughts. Low levels are shown in light colors, while heavy precipitation uses darker shades. Tracking this data helps with disaster preparedness, agriculture planning, and water resource management.

Temperature

Cold Hot

•Cold temperatures are represented by shades of blue.
•Moderate temperatures are represented by shades of green and yellow.
•Hot temperatures are represented by shades of orange and red.

Wind

Low High

Wind impacts temperature, humidity, and weather patterns, with strong winds often signaling storms or cold fronts. Represented by color gradients on maps, lighter shades indicate calm winds, and darker colors show stronger gusts. Wind data is essential for safe travel, outdoor activities, and tracking weather system movements.

Pressure

Low High

•Low pressure (associated with storms, rain, or bad weather) tends to be shown in blue/purple tones.
•High pressure (associated with clear skies, fair weather) is shown in yellow/orange/red tones.
